about me

My story of love for technology begins when I was a teen and got my first computer. Think white PC with CRT screen, 20GB of hard drive, less than 1GB RAM, Windows XP and no internet connection. Yeah, it was not much. But messing around with Windows made me think: "how the f*ck does it work?".

That was it! That was enough for me to pursue an entire career in tech.

I'm not even joking. The desire to know in every detail how Windows XP (and my computer) worked was what motived me to be in tech to this day. And getting the answer to this question was the most complex puzzle I had to solve. Which made me realize that:

Building an OS is HARDCORE.

But learning about it in college was fun. I wanted to build one by myself. But upon the realization that it might take a little more knowledge than I had, I got distracted. I found myself messing around with web dev, mobile, front-end, devops and whatnot...

I put OS dev on the back-burner.

I still want to develop an OS, just for fun. But my love for development is no longer attached only to it. So in the mean time, I'm helping some open source projects, worked for some companies and you can see it all on my resume. Or just keep scrolling.
